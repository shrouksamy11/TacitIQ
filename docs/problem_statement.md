# Problem Statement

## Background

Modern manufacturing companies depend heavily on experienced technicians who develop valuable practical knowledge over many years of work.

This knowledge is often based on experience rather than written procedures. Technicians recognize unusual sounds, smells, vibrations, or machine behaviors that may indicate future failures long before traditional monitoring systems detect them.

Unfortunately, this knowledge is rarely documented in a structured way.

When experienced employees retire, resign, or move to another company, years of operational expertise disappear with them.

As a result, organizations repeatedly face the same problems, spend additional time diagnosing known issues, and lose valuable maintenance knowledge that could have been reused.

## The Problem

Current maintenance systems mainly focus on sensor data, machine logs, and historical failures.

However, they largely ignore one of the most valuable sources of information:

Human observations and practical experience.

There is currently no simple and intelligent system that captures, organizes, connects, and reuses technicians' tacit knowledge as a permanent organizational asset.
